Shape Theory and Balance: Completed Flyer

This flyer was presented to the Dean of Student Life at BYU as part of a Social Impact class Spring 2022 while joint-interning at the Utah County Health Department. My team's job was two-fold: discover the breadth and depth of food scarcity among BYU students, and to inform students of available resources. As a result of this project, BYU now has canvas flyers all over campus based from my design and our team's research. Showcases my ability to complete a high-quality product from a mock-up and my eye for balance using geometric and freeform shapes. Designed on Canva.

Design Efficiency: Acoustic Night Flyer

Going for an autmunal semi-formal vibe, I created this flyer to motif the atmosphere of the night: semi-formal yet casual look, with formal writing and tone. I intended for this design to subtly communicate to people this message: Come dressed autumnal and semi-formally for a professional and cozy musical experience. This flyer has dual-purpose design: as an invite to guests and an invite to musicians. Highlights my abillity to design to multiple audiences and to influence action through design. Designed using an app.

Font and Color Variation: Utah County Health Department Ecosystem Map

This graphic is intended to visualize our research of UCHD's reach and role among it's market competitors and users. Designed on Notability, and with a two day time-constraint for reseach and design, I experimented with different hues, shades, and tints of blue. I also included font design by alternating Serif and Sans Serif.

Humor: English Class Meme

This is one of my first graphics I ever created. I used a template from an online source and one of my photos I took of the participants. I tried to minimize the words on the flyer to key sound bites and ideas that would resonate with moms and their kids, which were our target market.
